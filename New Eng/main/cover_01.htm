The oxidative stress, induced by the hepatitis B virus pre-S mutant large surface protein, was examined by Comet assay, which detects the DNA strand breaks. The DNA repair enzymeformamidepyrimidine-DNA glycosylase (FPG), which cleaves DNA at the sites of oxidative DNA lesion 8-hydroxyguanine, was employed to create DNA strand breaks at the sites attacked by oxidative stress. The DNA strand breakages were identified by single-cell electrophoresis, followed by SyBr Green staining. The tail moments shown above represent the levels of oxidative DNA damages.
