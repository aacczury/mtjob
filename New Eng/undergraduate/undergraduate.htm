  8. Clinical Microscopy (Including Lab)
    Clinical microscopy introduces the basic principles and skills of the routine examinations in out-patient department. It includes all the examinations of urine, feces, sputum, semen, body fluid, cerebrospinal fluid, gastric juice and bile analysis, pregnancy test, and synopsis of clinical biochemistry and microscopy. (Young, Kung-Chia)
  9. Microbiology (Including Lab)
  10. Immunology (Including Lab)
  11. Hematology

    The goal of the course is to teach students basic concepts of hematology. The students will learn to understand the physiology, composition and functions of blood, and the reasoning of routine examinations. The curriculum includes hematopoiesis, hemokinetics, hematopathology, normal and abnormal morphology of the blood cells, and hematostatic mechanism.
    This course is designed to teach the mechanisms, clinical features, and laboratory diagnosis of common hematological diseases. The topics will include anemias, acute and chromic leukemia, malignant lymphoma, multiple myeloma, platelet disorders and coagulation abnormality. (Lin, Tsun-Mei)

  12. Pathology
  13. Blood Banking (Including Lab)

    The object of this course is to teach students the general knowledge of the blood transfusion medicine, blood typing including blood groups, blood bank techniques, transfusion therapy, preparation of blood components and quality control in blood bank. (Lin,Tsune-Mein)

  14. Clinical Hematology Lab
    The course is designed to teach students the principles and techniques of blood routine examinations. It comprises skin-puncture, venipuncture, complete blood cell counting, blood cell morphology, preparation and staining of blood smear, special stains, laboratory diagnosis of hemorrhagic disorders, and cross test. (Lin, Tsun-Mei)
  15. Clinical Physiology
    The object of this course is to teach student the principles and clinical applications of electrocardiography, pulmonary function test, ultrasonography and neurophysiological tests. (Chang, Jen-Chyi)
  16. Clinical Serology and Immunology

    Application of immunological principles and methods to detect pathogens and to diagnose diseases is the main topic of this course. (Yeh, Trai - Ming)

  17. Clinical Serology and Immunology Lab
    Understand the properties of antigens and antibodies and learn different methods to detect antigens and antibodies.
  18. Clinical Biochemistry Lab
  19. Clinical Microbiology Lab
    The students of this class are expected to learn the methodology of isolation and identification of various pathogens in clinical specimens, and to recognize the function of clinical microbiology laboratory in nosocomial infection control. (Wu, Jiunn-Jong; Chang, Tsung-Chain; Chang, Jen-Chyi)
  20. Clinical Biochemistry

    This course encompasses the investigation of the chemical aspects of human life in health and disease. It teaches students to apply the chemical analysis of body fluids and other biological materials in the prevention, diagnosis and therapy of disease. (Shiesh, Shu-Chu)

  21. Clinical Microbiology

    This course concerns clinical bacteriology mainly. The topics include the culture and identification of common pathgens, methods of detect microorganisms in infections diseases, methods of examination and survey in nosocomial infection and antimicrobial susceptibility tests in clinical microbiology. (Wu, Jiunn-Jong;Chang, Tsung-Chain;Chang, Jen-Chyi)

  22. Clinical Virology
    This course will learn clinical and diagnosis virology. The students will acquire knowledges and update techniques for rapid and accurate laboratory diagnosis of medically important viruses and chlamydiae. (Wang, Jen-Ren)
  23. Clinical Microscopy Practice
  24. Clinical Physiology Practice
    The contents of this course include: (1) cardiac function tests, (2) pulmonary function tests, (3) encephalic and neural function tests. (Chang, Jen-Chyi)
  25. Clinical Biochemistry Practice
    This course is a clinical rotation in Clinical Biochemistry Laboratory to apply the theoretical knowledge to the actual laboratory situation. It also includes instrumentation, quality assurance and data interpretation. The student is expected to actively participate in the performance of every test, seminar and reading of procedure manuals and study guides. (Shiesh, Shu-Chu; Huang, Huei-Sheng)
  26. Clinical Hematology Practice

    The students of this class will have the opportunities to practice clinical hematology examinations and to operate automatic instruments in hematology laboratory. Topics of this course will include special hematological tests, laboratory diagnosis of hemolytic anemia, cytochemical stains of leukocytes, differentiation of bone marrow cells, and differential diagnosis of blood diseases. (Young, Kung-Chia, Huang, Wenya)

  27. Blood Banking Practice
    The student taking this course will practice the basic techniques in blood bank including blood grouping cross matching, antibody screening and blood components preparation and storage. The object of this course is to teach students the function of blood banking in medicine. (Lin,Tsune-Mein)
  28. Clinical Microbiology Practice
    The students of this class are expected to learn the methodology of isolation and identification of various pathogens in clinical specimens, and to recognize the function of clinical microbiology laboratory in nosocomial infection control. (Wu, Jiunn-Jong; Chang, Tsung-Chain; Chang, Jen-Chyi)
  29. Clinical Virology Practice
    Laboratory exercises will emphasize both conventional and newly developed techniques, including viral isolation and identification by cell cultures, Hemagglutination, hemadsorption, immunofluorescence, enzyme immunoassay and viral nucleic acid detection.(Wang, Jen-Ren)
  30. Clinical Serology and Immunology Practice

    Introduce the clinical applications of serological and immunological methods to diagnose diseases. (Yeh, Trai-Ming)

  31. Cytogenetics Practice
    The students of this class will be trained on the following techniques: (1)cell culture, (2)slide preparation, (3)chromosome observation, (4)karyotyping.
  32. Laboratory Quality Control

    The accuracy and precision of laboratory data influences directly on clinical diagnosis and therapy. This course describes the basic concepts of quality control-- the quality control in personal environment and instruments, and the total quality control programs of laboratory. (Shiesh, Shu-Chu)

  33. Seminars
    The purpose in this course is to train oral and written skills of a scientific presentation. Discussion, study and oral presentation on a particular topic under mentor instruction is required for each student.

The learning objectives include (1) understanding the basic molecules of a cell – the protein, DNA, RNA molecules (2) understanding the functional structures of a cell – the membrane, nucleus, mitochondria (3) how the cooperative systems in a cell to generate energy, grow, divide, move and responds to stimuli (4) the experimental designs and skills in the research of cell biology (5) problem-based discussion and presentation.(Young, Kung-Chia, Huang, Wenya;)

  19. Advanced Immunology
    This course is for students who have taken microbiology and still want to learn more about immunology .We will discuss some hot topics in current immunology.Students are required to present immunology related papers and attend seminars.For those who are interested to get into graduate school of immunology,this course will certainly help you to familiar with graduate study. (Yeh, Trai-Ming)
  20. Medical Genetics
  21. Molecular Diagnosis In Genetic Diseases
  22. Molecular Diagnosis for Viral Hepatitis

    The learning objectives of this course include (1) the preparation of samples, materials, reagents and working area for molecular diagnostic laboratory (2) the principles and methods for quanlitative and quantitative measurement of hepatitis B virus (HBV) DNA and hepatitis C virus (HCV) RNA (3) the principles and methods for identification of viral genotypes and drug-resistant variant strains (4) the clinical applications of molecular diagnosis on HBV and HCV (5) the development of molecular diagnostic kits for HBV and HCV (6) hand-on experiences for molecular diagnostic of HBV and HCV (7) problem-based discussion and presentation. .(Young, Kung-Chia)
  23. Clinical Mycology(Including Lab)

    There are lectures, laboratory, and clinical practice in the 'clinical Mycology' course.The contents of lectures include physiology, reproductive, and classification of fungi; fungi-related-diseases and their differentiation; all groups of antifungal agents and their use.Students will learn how to differentiate and identify medically important fungi and contaminants in labs.In clinical practice, students will be arranged to follow a dermatologist (Dr. Ming-Long Hsu) in his out-patient-clinics, to observe fungi-related-diseases and to learn sampling and direct examination from lesions in clinics.After studying the course, students should learn the prevalence, symptoms, diagnosis and treatment of common mycoses and be able to identify those common fungi isolated in clinical labs. (Chang, Jen-Chyi；Hsu, Ming-Long)
